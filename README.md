# Cross-Asset-Correlations
This project involved developing a multivariate DCC‑GARCH framework to produce forward‑looking cross‑asset correlation forecasts and assess regime stability across equity and rates markets. The model generated scenario‑based correlation surfaces and simulated price paths to evaluate how correlations behaved under varying market conditions. The analysis ultimately revealed a pronounced correlation breakdown between S&P 500 E‑mini futures and 30‑Year U.S. Treasury Bond futures, with correlations declining from approximately 0.60 to 0.06 and tail dependence approaching zero (around 0.002). These findings suggested the emergence of a potential structural shift in the relationship between equity and interest‑rate markets.
